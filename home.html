A guide to help Graudate students navigate their academic life during their stay at the University of Michigan.

Study Spaces

Having a dedicated study space is important since it can directly tie into the success of a student's academics. It has the ability of enhancing focus, improving productivity, and reducing distractions. Depending on what a student needs, finding the right place to study can make a significant difference.

Reserving a Spot

Many areas on campus are free for students to come and go as they please, yet there are some rooms that require a reservation, so it is important to refer to the reservation sign up sheets.

  • Central Campus
  • North Campus
  • U-M Library
  • Detroit Center

Types of Study Spaces

There are designated areas for specific needs, in order to accomodate all students. These include:

  • Quiet Study Spaces: Quiet places to do more focused individual work.
  • Collaborative Study Spaces: For group work.
  • Soundproof Booths: Sound privacy.
  • Student Parent & Caregiver Study Rooms: Rooms dedicated to parents and caregivers

Mental Health

Maintaining your mental well-being is crucial for thriving in graduate school. Here are resources to support your mental health:

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS):

Access individual counseling, group therapy, workshops, and crisis intervention services.

Wellness Coaching:

Work with a wellness coach to develop strategies for managing stress, time, and overall well-being.

Mindfulness Resources:

Participate in mindfulness and meditation sessions to cultivate a calm and focused mind.

Support Groups:

Join peer support groups for shared experiences and collective coping strategies.

Campus Life & Extracurricular Activities

Balancing academics, work, and life can be a challenge, but it is important for graduate students especially, to find time to enjoy themselves. At the University of Michigan there are various resources for students that can help them find that necessary balance:

Student Organizations:
There are more than 1,400 student organizations on campus that focus on a variety of different interests.
Arts and Culture:
UM hosts year-round rich cultural events. From performing arts to museums and galleries.
Recreation:
The Department of Recreational Sports offers a wide range of activities for students in addition to the already three recreational buildings on campus.
Connect2Community:
Connect2Community provides students with opportunities to volunteer at schools, non-profits, and other agencies.
The City:
As a student, you have access to free bus transportation, where you can explore more than 400 restaurants, cultural sites, and more.
